Pilot Community Engagement Program

A hub of resources and tools for basic income pilot organizations and leaders to use in building successful pilots that maximize opportunities for deep community engagement.

Engage your community

Supporting pilot success and creating coalitions of advocates for basic income

Income Movement's Pilot Community Engagement Program (PCEP) offers pilots the tools, resources and direct support needed to engage their local community around guaranteed basic income.

The first goal for the PCE program is to support guaranteed income pilot organizations in developing long-term community coalitions in support of basic income. When a pilot ends, a thriving basic income community must remain to advocate for expanding the pilot to broader policy. Income Movement‚Äôs primary focus as an organization is to build grassroots coalitions in the fight for basic income. Through events, workshops, activities, direct action and more, we help pilot organizations educate their partners, participants and broader communities around the idea of basic income and connect the pilot to the national movement to create long-term engagement.
The second goal of the program is to support organizations running guaranteed income pilots with the tools and resources necessary to help them be successful and effective through each phase of the pilot process.

The toolkit found here is a collection of best practices from experts across the pilot sector that any organization who is interested in serving their community through a guaranteed income pilot can utilize. This is intended to be a flexible set of resources that will allow organizations to build thoughtfully and effectively while staying true to their unique vision. While Income Movement may be hosting and organizing this guide and toolkit, it is a tool to be owned, utilized, and expanded upon by the greater community of pilot organizations.

The Pilot Lifecycle

The model above shows the lifecycle of a traditional pilot. There are 5 distinct phases in a pilot's lifecycle that call on the pilot team to consider and build out different components. These phases are not entirely fixed or rigid but are helpful when discussing the processes that go into running a pilot program. One phase will inform and lead to the next but there will be some overlap between phases and, in some instances, you may need to return to a previous phase in order to troubleshoot anything not functioning properly. The structure of the lifecycle is how we have organized the toolkit as exemplified by the sub-navigation. Under each of these phases, you can find the different questions to consider along with guidance and tools created by the Income Movement team and submitted by pilot organizations. If you have any questions about the structure of the pilot lifecycle or want to dig deeper into it, connect with us by contacting our Initiative Manager Erica.

A Collective Effort

This guide and toolkit is an effective and efficient way for pilots from across the nation to come together and create a more unified movement for direct cash programs. Income Movement has worked with amazing pilot programs from across the country to help formalize the process for creating and conducting pilot programs. We have incorporated deep community engagement into the process that will have a ripple effect across the U.S., leading to state and national policy at an exponential rate.

We would like to extend a personal thank you to all pilot organizations and thought leadership that have contributed to this toolkit. We are deeply grateful to have you be a part of the creation of the resources found within.

If your organization is interested in providing feedback, sharing best practices or tools, or partnering with Income Movement, feel free to reach out to our Initiative Manager Erica
